# Program

## Program Overview

The RR-BIG Summer Institute is structured as an intensive, sequential three-week training program. Each day is organized into three pillars:

- Lecture (9:00 AM – 11:30 AM, 1:00 PM - 2:00 PM): Didactic theory, methodology, and mathematical formulations.

- Seminar (11:30 AM – 12:30 PM, M/W/F): Invited seminar talks with researcher leaders in imaging genetics.

- Hands-On Laboratory (2:30 PM – 3:30 PM): Active-learning coding, processing, and pipeline execution inside the secure MSI cluster sandbox.

## Week 1: Neuroimaging Foundations

- Focus: Understanding brain structures, transforming raw structural and functional MRI data into standard, quality-controlled, and topologically accurate phenotypes.

- Leader Instructor and Lab Mentor: Dr. Eric Feczko 

- Seminar Speakers: Drs. Sylia Wilson, Tervo-Clemmens, and Steve Nelson

- Topics: brain anatomy, MRI terminology, BIDS, preprocessing, phenotype extraction, quality control, and precision functional mapping.

## Week 2: Genomics and Statistical Genetics

- Focus: Processing genetic datasets, controlling for ancestral confounding, estimating heritability, and executing genome-wide association studies (GWAS)

- Leader Instructor: Dr. Saonli Basu 

- Lab Mentors: Dr. Christian Coffman and Mr. Kody DeGolier

- Seminar Speakers: Drs. Wei Pan, Brendon Coombes, and Jin Jin

- Topics: genetic QC, GRM, population stratification, ancestry, heritability estimation, GWAS, and polygenic risk score modeling.

## Week 3: Integrative ABCD Analysis and Reproducibility

- Focus: Executing safe, compliant, and fully reproducible neuroimaging genetics analyses on the ABCD study.

- Leader Instructor: Dr. Lin Zhang 

- Lab Mentor: Dr. Kelly Duffy

- Seminar Speakers: Drs. Tianzhong Yang, Thierry Chekouo, and David Kennedy

- Topics: study design, data curation, capstone project on ABCD study, visualization, and reproducible documentation.
