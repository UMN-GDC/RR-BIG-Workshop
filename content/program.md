# Program

## Program Overview

The RR-BIG Summer Institute is structured as an intensive, sequential three-week training program. Each day is organized into three pillars:

- **Lectures (9:00 AM - 10:30 AM, 1:00 PM - 2:00 PM):** Didactic theory, methodology, and mathematical foundations.
- **Seminars / roundtables (11:30 AM - 12:30 PM, Monday/Wednesday/Friday):** Discussion sessions with invited speakers and faculty.
- **Hands-on laboratory (2:30 PM - 3:30 PM):** Active-learning coding, processing, and pipeline execution.

## Learning approach

The institute is designed to move from concepts to implementation. Each week combines scientific motivation, methodological background, practical computing, and discussion of real-world challenges in neuroimaging genomics.

## Core learning outcomes

- Understand major questions in brain imaging genomics and mental health research
- Use reproducible workflows for neuroimaging and genomics data analysis
- Interpret ancestry, relatedness, heritability, and PRS methods in diverse cohorts
- Connect classroom methods to collaborative, team-based research projects

## Week 1: Neuroimaging Foundations

- **Focus:** Understanding brain structures and transforming raw structural and functional MRI data into meaningful, quality-controlled phenotypes.
- **Lead instructor:** Dr. Eric Feczko
- **Topics:** brain anatomy, MRI terminology, BIDS, preprocessing, phenotype extraction, quality control, and precision functional mapping

## Week 2: Genomics and Statistical Genetics

- **Focus:** Processing genetic datasets, controlling for relatedness and ancestral confounding, estimating heritability, and evaluating polygenic prediction.
- **Lead instructor:** Dr. Saonli Basu
- **Lab mentors:** Dr. Christian Coffman and Mr. Kody DeGolier
- **Topics:** genetic data foundations, kinship and GRM, population stratification, ancestry inference, admixture, heritability estimation, and polygenic risk scores

### Week 2 daily structure

- **Day 1:** Genetic data foundations for brain imaging genetics, genotype data formats, QC concepts, and statistical genetics motivation
- **Day 2:** Relatedness, kinship estimation, mixed effect models, and genomic relationship matrices
- **Day 3:** Population stratification, ancestry inference, admixture, and nonlinear methods including PCA, UMAP, and VAE
- **Day 4:** Heritability estimation in family-based and SNP-based settings, with attention to multi-site and multi-ancestry studies
- **Day 5:** Polygenic risk score analysis, evaluation, portability, and statistical and machine learning approaches in diverse samples

## Week 3: Integrative ABCD Analysis and Reproducibility

- **Focus:** Executing safe, compliant, and reproducible neuroimaging genetics analyses in a team science setting.
- **Lead instructor:** Dr. Lin Zhang
- **Topics:** study design, data curation, phenotype selection, downstream analysis, visualization, and reproducible documentation
